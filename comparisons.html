Latitude vs. Max Temperature

After plotting each city's latitude vs. its max temperature, there appears to be an obvious trend showing the temperature dropping as the latitude grows larger (on the positive side).

Latitude vs. Humidity

The graph above appears to show there is very little direct correlation between a city's latitude and its humidity levels. Interestingly, however, the graph shows that some locations near the North Pole were bone dry while others at the same latitude were drenched. The only trend that can be pulled from viewing this graph is that nearly all cities within the Tropics have at least 20 percent humidity, which goes along with common knowledge.

Latitude vs. Cloudiness

Again, there appears to be little correlation between latitude and cloudiness. As seen in the graph, many locations at all latitudes had no cloudiness and the distribution of latitudes looks relatively even as cloudiness increases.
